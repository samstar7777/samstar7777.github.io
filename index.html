The Pilot of

the Millenium Falcon

This is a funny little story from when I first came here and started teaching English.

It takes awhile to get used to communicating in english with people whose second language is english. when you do it all the time for a long period, you learn well how to do it, but when you first start--- you make a lot of mistakes. One funny moment from my first year here was when I was talking to a group of 4 students about Star Wars. Somehow Han Solo came up as a topic (I am not sure if I was trying to tell them about the time I was a cook in New Orleans and made food for Harrison Ford and made an excuse to return some gorgonzola to the cheese closet at the side of the bar so I could get a peek at him. There he was, eating his pizza. he was in town filming Looper). Anyway, they did not know who Han Solo was... I tried to think "how can I explain?" I got really excited "...he's the pilot of the Millenium Falcon!" You don't need to be Galen Erso to figure out that if a group of people don't know a certain fictional character inside a movie, that they have almost zero chance of knowing the name of the spaceship they pilot. The teacher teaching in the room next to me overheard and talked to me about it in the teachers room between lessons. We had a good laugh and I think he suggested that I mention the fact that “It's the ship that made the Kessel Run in less than twelve parsecs!”
